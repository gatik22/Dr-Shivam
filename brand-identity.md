## 5. Tone of Voice

### Communication Principles

| Principle | Description | тЬЕ Do | тЭМ Don't |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Confident Authority** | Experienced surgeon who inspires trust | "With over 3,000 successful surgeriesтАж" | "We think we might be able to helpтАж" |
| **Clear & Reassuring** | Patients are anxious about surgery тАФ reassure them | "You'll be walking within 48 hours of surgery" | "Complex tibial plateau fixation with LCP" |
| **Empathetic** | Acknowledge the patient's pain and frustration | "We understand that living with joint pain affects every part of your life" | "You should have come in sooner" |
| **Outcome-Focused** | Paint a picture of life after treatment | "Imagine climbing stairs without pain again" | "We perform osteotomies" |
| **Honest** | Be upfront about surgery, recovery, and expectations | "Recovery takes 6тАУ8 weeks with dedicated physiotherapy" | "You'll be 100% in no time" |

### Writing Style
- **Sentence case** for all headings (not Title Case)
- **British English** preferred (orthopaedic, specialised, personalised)
- **Short paragraphs** тАФ 2тАУ3 sentences max
- **Active voice** тАФ "We restore your mobility" not "Your mobility is restored by us"
- **Second person** тАФ Speak to "you" directly
- **Use concrete outcomes** тАФ "walking within 48 hours", "back on the pitch in 6 months"

### Brand Vocabulary

**Use these words often:**
> Orthopaedic ┬╖ Joint ┬╖ Mobility ┬╖ Movement ┬╖ Precision ┬╖ Minimally invasive ┬╖ Arthroscopy ┬╖ Implant ┬╖ Recovery ┬╖ Rehabilitation ┬╖ Pain-free ┬╖ Restore ┬╖ Strength ┬╖ Surgical expertise ┬╖ Fracture ┬╖ Spine ┬╖ Evidence-based ┬╖ Advanced techniques

**Avoid these words:**
> Cheap ┬╖ Quick-fix ┬╖ Guaranteed ┬╖ Revolutionary ┬╖ Miracle ┬╖ Cutting (too literal for surgery) ┬╖ Alternative ┬╖ Experimental ┬╖ Risky

### Orthopaedic-Specific Messaging

| Patient Concern | Reassuring Message |
|---|---|
| Fear of surgery | "Minimally invasive techniques mean smaller incisions, less pain, and faster recovery" |
| Long recovery worry | "Our structured physiotherapy programmes get you back on your feet sooner" |
| Cost anxiety | "We offer transparent pricing and will discuss all options with you upfront" |
| Age-related hesitation | "Age is not a barrier тАФ we regularly help patients in their 70s and 80s return to active lives" |
| Wanting to avoid surgery | "Surgery is always the last resort. We explore all non-surgical options first" |

## 6. Imagery & Visual Style

### Photography Direction

| Category | Style | Mood |
|---|---|---|
| **Clinic / OT** | Clean, modern, well-equipped operating theatre, warm waiting areas | Reassuring, not intimidating |
| **Surgeon Portrait** | Warm lighting, confident posture, white coat + stethoscope | Trustworthy, skilled |
| **Medical / Orthopaedic** | X-ray imagery styled artistically, implant close-ups, healthy joints | Scientific, precise |
| **Patient Recovery** | Walking again, physiotherapy, returning to sports/daily life | Empowering, hopeful |
| **Equipment** | Premium implants, arthroscopic tools, surgical instruments тАФ shot beautifully | Advanced, premium |

### Photography Rules
1. **Natural, warm lighting** тАФ avoid harsh clinical fluorescents
2. **Soft, desaturated colour grading** тАФ match the sage/cream palette
3. **Show recovery moments** тАФ patients walking, exercising, smiling post-surgery
4. **Diverse ages** in patient imagery (sports injuries in youth, joint replacements in elderly)
5. **Never show graphic surgical images** in marketing material
6. **X-rays and scans can be used decoratively** тАФ desaturated, as background elements

### Image Treatment
- Slight **brightness reduction** and **desaturation** for background images
- **Rounded corners** (`24px` / `32px`) on all image containers
- **Layered/stacked compositions** тАФ overlapping images with white card borders
- **Subtle shadows** тАФ never harsh drop shadows; use layered box-shadows

## 11. Brand Do's & Don'ts

### тЬЕ Do
- Use the italic accent pattern in headings
- Maintain generous whitespace тАФ let the content breathe
- Show patients in recovery and returning to activities they love
- Mention specific outcomes (walking within 48 hours, return to sport in 6 months)
- Use "orthopaedic" (British spelling, the accepted medical standard)
- Speak to patients as partners in their recovery journey
- Reference surgical volume and success rates to build trust

### тЭМ Don't
- Use graphic surgical imagery in patient-facing materials
- Use neon or saturated greens
- Use pure black or harsh shadows
- Use complex medical jargon without explanation
- Promise guaranteed outcomes or "miracle cures"
- Use stock images of people who are clearly not in pain or recovery
- Mix in unrelated accent colours (blues, reds, purples)
- Make the brand feel clinical and cold тАФ warmth is the differentiator
